I ran a two sample t test and cohen’s d test on female and male hare weights. There was a significant different in body mass, and a small effect size (Cohen’s d = -0.2904674). The resulting degrees of freedom was 325.02. The p value, the probability that we could have gotten sample means that are least as different as these if the null hypothesis (that they were pulled from populations with the same mean) was true is 0.007093. This means there is a .7% chance we could have gotten these from random chance if they were pulled from populations from the same mean, showing that sex probably has a significant effect on the weight of the hares. The cohen.d test showed us that the d estimate, the effect size, was -0.2904674, a small effect size of the sex of the hare on their weight.
